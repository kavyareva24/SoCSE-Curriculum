**COURSE OVERVIEW:**

The field of reinforcement learning (RL) is rapidly evolving, and numerous courses are available for learners at different levels. Here's an overview of some prominent courses that cover various aspects of reinforcement learning.

**COURSE OBJECTIVE (S):**

The Objective of this course are to

- Introduce the foundational principles, terminology, and motivation behind Reinforcement Learning and Intelligent Systems.
- Compare reinforcement learning with supervised and unsupervised learning paradigms, highlighting their applications and limitations.
- Develop a clear understanding of Markov Decision Processes and their role in sequential decision-making.
- Explain policy formation, value functions, and the Bellman equation as applied in deterministic and stochastic environments.
- Demonstrate the working of Q-learning algorithms through real-world examples and simulations.
- To identify and analyze various intelligent agent architectures and their interaction with different environments using the PEAS framework

**COURSE CONTENT:**

**UNIT-1**

**Introduction to Reinforcement Learning:** Early Roots of Reinforcement Learning, Need for Reinforcement Learning, Difference Between Reinforcement Learning, Supervised Learning and Unsupervised Learning, Terms Used in Reinforcement Learning: Elements of Reinforcement Learning, Types of Environments, Working of Reinforcement Learning, Approaches To Implementing Reinforcement Learning, Types of Reinforcement Learning, Exploration Vs Exploitation Trade-Off, Advantages of Reinforcement Learning, Applications of Reinforcement Learning, Challenges with Reinforcement Learning, Reinforcement Learning Vs Deep Learning Vs Machine Learning.

**UNIT-2**

**Markov Decision Process:** Understanding MDP: Key Components Of MDP, Formal Definition Of MDP; Markov Property: Markov Chain, Key Concepts and Analyses Associated with Markov Chains, Markov Model, Markov Matrix, Using Markov Matrices in Machine Learning; Markov Decision Process Framework: Reward and Returns, Markov Reward Process; Value Function in MDP: State Value Function, Action Value Function; Optimal Policy In MDP, Examples of MDP.

UNIT-3

**Policy In MDP, Q-Learning:** Introduction, Types of Policy: Deterministic Policy, Stochastic Policy; Bellman Equation for Value Function, Recycling Robot: Case Study, Optimal Policy and Value Function, Backup Diagram, Overview Of Q-Learning, Terms in Q Learning with Notations, Working Of Q-Learning, Gambler in A Dungeon Example, Application of Q Learning, Case Study Examples of Q Learning.

UNIT-4

**Introduction to Intelligent Systems and agents:** Introduction to Intelligent Systems: Definition and Evolution of Intelligent Systems, Characteristics of Intelligent Behavior, Difference between Traditional and Intelligent Systems, Applications. Intelligent Agents and Environments: PEAS Framework, Types of Agents: Simple Reflex, Model-Based, Goal-Based, Utility-Based, Learning Agents, Environment types, Agent types. Environments.
